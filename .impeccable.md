## Design Context

### Users
AI researchers, policymakers, academics, journalists, and hiring decision-makers. They arrive to assess credibility and depth of work — often time-limited. The job: form a confident impression of who Avijit is, find specific publications or materials, and contact him.

### Brand Personality
**Clean. Serious. Sharp.**
Not sterile — authoritative. Substance over decoration. The kind of site a thoughtful academic would trust immediately.

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial minimalism. Libre Franklin as the single font family, exploiting its weight range (300–700) to create hierarchy through contrast, not decoration. Generous whitespace. Typographic structure replaces chrome. No cards unless truly necessary. Color used sparingly — theme accent as a sharp signal, not ambiance.

**Anti-references**: Cluttered academic sites with too many sections, gradient-heavy "personal brand" sites, anything that feels templated or AI-generated.

### Design Principles
1. **Subtract before you add.** If an element isn't communicating something specific, remove it.
2. **Typography is the structure.** Weight, size, and spacing carry the hierarchy — not borders, cards, or shadows.
3. **Whitespace is not waste.** Generous margins signal confidence.
4. **One color rule.** The theme accent appears to point, not to decorate.
5. **Libre Franklin first.** Use its 300–700 weight range for all hierarchy. No second font needed.
